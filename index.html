The Wound That Became a Presidency

A pattern analysis. Not a diagnosis.
by Christian J. Charette, LMFT
from The Father Wounds That Shaped the World
This is not about one man. This is about what happens when a wound is handed a microphone, a crowd, and the nuclear codes.
Fred Trump told his sons to be killers. Not metaphorically. Not as a pep talk. As doctrine.
PBS Frontline, "compete, win, be a killer… do what you have to do to win"
The message was different for the boys than for the girls. The boys were told to dominate. The first son who refused, Fred Jr., chose flying over real estate, chose freedom over the family machine, and drank himself dead at forty-two.
The second son who complied became president.
Britannica, Fred urged his son to be a "killer" in anything he pursued
His mother became seriously ill when he was two and a half. Not old enough to name what was happening. Old enough to feel it.
"When you ask him about how she showed her love, he has nothing to say."
PBS Frontline, Mary Trump on Donald's mother's absence at a critical developmental stage
So the father filled the vacuum. Not with warmth. With instruction. Win. Dominate. Never show need.
At thirteen, he was sent to military school. For the first time in his life, someone slapped him when he stepped out of line.
He calls it a rite of passage. A clinician calls it a second abandonment.
If a man has a father wound, the boy inside him will remain frozen, forever auditioning for a father who never clapped.
When he cannot secure love, he mistakes dominance for approval, control for safety, fear for respect.
He does not grow an inner compass. He replaces it with mirrors.
NPR, "The leader must be everywhere, his face must be everywhere, his name must be everywhere"
Adornment becomes identity. Gold replaces warmth. Applause replaces attunement. Wealth becomes proof of worth instead of a tool.
PBS, East Wing demolished for a $400M ballroom. A 250-foot arch near Arlington. His signature on U.S. currency. His name on the Kennedy Center. Axios, A gold commemorative coin. His face on a banner hanging from the Justice Department. Airport trademark filings. Penn Station. Dulles. House Judiciary Democrats, $11.6 billion in crypto holdings. $800 million in profits in six months. Top 220 memecoin holders invited to dinner with the president.
Because if he stops shining, he disappears.
He learns early: weakness is punished. Tenderness is ignored. Need is dangerous.
So he kills the boy and builds a brand.
He worships power because power feels like protection. He confuses authority with attachment. He confuses loyalty with love. And because love was conditional, he makes loyalty absolute.
Only sycophants are safe. Only praise is tolerated. Criticism feels like annihilation.
Not disagreement. Abandonment.
Not feedback. Humiliation.
January 2025, 17 independent watchdogs fired by email in a single night March 2026, Kristi Noem fired as DHS Secretary after contradicting him on camera April 2, 2026, Pam Bondi fired as Attorney General for failing to prosecute his enemies fast enough
So he attacks. He dominates. He mocks. He belittles.
Rage becomes his armor, because rage keeps grief out.
A man with an unhealed father wound does not lead. He polices.
And because genuine connection was never learned, he replaces it with manipulation.
What looks like deal-making is really control management.
Negotiation becomes leverage. Conversation becomes transaction. Relationship becomes a zero-sum game.
There must be a winner. And he must always be it.
He is obsessed with fairness, not justice.
Fairness asks: Did I lose? Justice asks: Who was harmed?
Fairness keeps score. Justice requires perspective.
Fairness protects his ego. Justice would threaten it.
So he fixates. Counts. Audits. Recalculates.
Because fairness soothes the wound that says: I was cheated.
Justice would ask a harder question: Who am I responsible to?
He must always be the smartest because being ignorant once meant being small.
A perfect brain. Perfect health. Perfect strength.
Perfection is not pride here. It's defense.
Admitting fault would mean admitting need. Admitting need would mean returning to the boy who begged silently and was ignored.
So he cannot lose.
Loss is intolerable. Failure is unthinkable. Being wrong feels like erasure.
When confronted with facts, he does not integrate them. He overrides them.
Loss becomes fraud. Defeat becomes victory. Contradiction becomes conspiracy.
And when reality resists him, he doubles down, then triples down, then demands loyalty as proof of truth.
When the Supreme Court rules against him, he calls his own appointees "disloyal to the Constitution." He calls the decision "almost like not written by smart people." He calls justices Barrett and Gorsuch "an embarrassment to their families."
NBC, Trump calls Supreme Court justices "disloyal" and "unpatriotic" after tariff ruling TIME, Trump calls the Court "a weaponized and unjust Political Organization" Truthout, The administration defied approximately 1 in 3 court rulings against it
Because truth is no longer factual. It is relational.
If you agree with him, it is real. If you challenge him, you are disloyal.
2025, Nearly 300,000 federal workers removed. Loyalty tests embedded in 5,800 job listings. Pew Research, Federal workforce shrank 10.3% in one year. USAID gutted by 92%. CDC lost a third of staff.
Here is the paradox.
The same wound that demands dominance creates a fatal weakness.
He is exquisitely vulnerable to flattery.
Praise bypasses scrutiny. Adoration disarms judgment. Sycophancy feels like safety.
TIME, Jan 2026, World leaders now treat managing Trump's emotions as a strategic priority CNN, Foreign leaders turn the sycophancy up to eleven
The NATO Secretary General called him "daddy." A Swiss delegation brought a gold bar. Qatar gifted a $400 million airplane. Pakistan nominated him for the Nobel. Seven countries followed.
Those who praise him are trusted. Those who challenge him are removed.
Loyalty is rewarded over competence. Because competence can disagree. Loyalty cannot.
FBI leadership gutted. Senior national security ranks "decimated."
This is not strategy. It is dependency.
He cannot tell the difference between being respected and being mirrored.
He manipulates to stay on top. Others manipulate him by telling him he already is.
Foreign Affairs, Flattery quickly loses value as more leaders deploy it. But they keep deploying it.
Grandiosity is not confidence. It is compensation.
Language never matures because reflection was never modeled. Empathy never develops because it was never received.
Asked if there were any limits on his global powers, he said:
"My own morality. My own mind. It's the only thing that can stop me."
ABC News, Jan 8, 2026, "I don't need international law. I'm not looking to hurt people." Anadolu, Asked if his power was limited by the Constitution: "We'll never get to the courts. We'll never get to the Constitution."
A man whose father taught him that need is weakness and whose mother's absence taught him that love disappears, that man does not develop morality. He develops strategy.
And he calls it the same thing.
And this is where humanity disappears.
When a man never learns to be seen, he never learns how to see.
Other people become abstractions, statistics, threats, invaders.
The Fulcrum, "animals, not human," "savages," "bad genes," "poisoning the blood"
Immigrants are not families. They are numbers. They are stains. They are problems to be removed.
Marshall Project, March 2026, A deaf six-year-old deported without his hearing devices Vera Institute, 32 died in ICE custody in 2025. 65% of detainees had no criminal convictions. Because recognizing their humanity would require accessing his own.
And that door was welded shut long ago.
Hierarchy replaces empathy. Order replaces compassion. Cruelty becomes efficiency.
If you never learned mercy, you mistake it for weakness.
American Immigration Council, $170 billion for enforcement. ICE now the highest-funded law enforcement agency in U.S. history. NPR, 115 people killed in Caribbean boat strikes. Fishermen. Laborers. Human Rights Watch, "Unlawful extrajudicial killings." Survivors clinging to wreckage struck again.
And the mother wound lingers too.
A mother who could not, or would not, protect him leaves a split that never resolves.
Women become either trophies to consume or enemies to punish.
Adoration is demanded. Challenge is met with rage.
Because when a woman questions him, the child hears betrayal, not dialogue.

This is not cruelty performed for cameras. This is what cruelty looks like when no one is supposed to be watching.
The boy inside the man was never allowed to need anything. So he cannot fathom that another child's need might be sacred.

A man who was never given anything freely grows up believing everything must be taken.
Countries are not neighbors. They are acquisitions. Greenland is "psychologically needed for success." Cuba is "ready to fall." Venezuela is "something we'll run." Colombia's president needs to "watch his ass."

Ownership is the only language the wound understands. Because the boy was never owned by love. He was owned by expectation. And now he owns everything he touches, or destroys it trying.

The question is not whether the wound is real. The evidence is public. The pattern is documented. The consequences are measurable, and tonight, they may become irreversible.

What wound are you governing from, and who is paying the price?
